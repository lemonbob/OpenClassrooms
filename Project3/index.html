Located at Pier 1 of Brooklyn Bridge Park, the Brooklyn Outdoor Film Festival is set against the stunning panoramic views of lower Manhatten.

With a 40 foot giant screen, and room for 5000 people, come and soak up the atmosphere. We have a mix of films from some of the latest releases to classics, all suitable for the whole family — what better place to enjoy a warm summer evening.

Tickets are free, but please pre-register your place by clicking on the golden ticket above as the event is extremely popular!

Brooklyn Bridge Park is an 85-acre post-industrial waterfront site stretching 1.3 miles along Brooklyn’s East River edge. The site spans from Jay Street, north of the Manhattan Bridge, to Pier 6 and Atlantic Avenue in the south. The site is long and narrow with a crenulated edge formed by its reclaimed piers. The park, designed by Michael Van Valkenburgh Associates, Inc., includes Piers 1-6, Empire Fulton Ferry, John Street and Main Street. In addition, two historic properties, the Civil war-era Empire Stores and the Tobacco Warehouse, have been integrated into the park.
